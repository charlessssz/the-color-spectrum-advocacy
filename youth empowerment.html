üî• DEFINING YOUTH EMPOWERMENT

Youth empowerment is the process of providing young people with the skills, opportunities, resources, and voice needed to participate meaningfully in decisions that affect their lives and communities.

When young people are empowered, they grow into leaders, innovators, and changemakers who strengthen democratic participation and contribute to building resilient, future-ready communities.
Source: National Youth Commission ‚Äì Youth Participation Framework

üåà THE FIVE PILLARS OF YOUTH EMPOWERMENT

Our approach is guided by both global and local youth development frameworks, focusing on the systems and conditions needed to empower every young Filipino to lead and succeed.

1. Access and Opportunity

Expanding equitable access to quality education, decent employment pathways, and comprehensive health and well-being services.

2. Voice and Participation

Strengthening youth engagement in policy-making, local governance, and national development by ensuring their perspectives are included and valued.

3. Skill Development

Building critical thinking, technical expertise, civic literacy, and ethical leadership to help young people navigate and address complex social challenges.

4. Safety and Well-being

Promoting policies and environments that protect young people, support mental health, and uphold safe, inclusive, and non-discriminatory spaces.

5. Ownership and Responsibility

Fostering accountability, community involvement, and a strong sense of responsibility for long-term national progress and sustainability.

üõë CRITICAL CHALLENGES FACING FILIPINO YOUTH

True empowerment requires confronting the systemic barriers that disproportionately affect young Filipinos, especially those in marginalized sectors.

Economic & Educational Disparity

High rates of "Not in Education, Employment, or Training" (NEET) among youth (estimated at 20-25%) due to lack of job opportunities and inadequate access to quality, relevant tertiary education.

Source: Philippine Institute for Development Studies (PIDS)
The Digital & Information Divide

While internet penetration is high, the gap in digital literacy and access to reliable information remains vast, making youth vulnerable to disinformation and limiting participation in the digital economy.

Source: Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT)
Intersectionality & Policy Gaps

Empowerment efforts must address intersectional identities, including Indigenous Peoples (IP) youth, LGBTQ+ youth, and young women, who face compounded barriers due to systemic discrimination and lack of protective policies.

Source: Human Rights Watch / Local NGO Reports
