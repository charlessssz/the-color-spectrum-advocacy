What is Voters Education?

VE is a process that goes beyond knowing how to shade a ballot. It equips you with the knowledge, skills, and ethical perspective to participate in elections responsibly.

  • Voters Education promotes an issue-based vote over a personality-based one.
  • It strengthens the constitutional basis of democratic participation.

Why Voters Education is Crucial

Voters Education is the foundation of a robust democracy, especially against issues like patronage politics and electoral fraud.

  • Accountability: Teaches voters to hold officials accountable based on their platforms and track record.
  • Fights Manipulation: Limits the power of 'money and guns' and reduces the effect of misinformation (fake news).

Qualifications & Disqualifications

To vote, you must be a Filipino citizen, 18 years old by Election Day, and meet the residency requirements (1 year in the Philippines, 6 months in your locality).

  • Disqualification: Final judgment of imprisonment of one year or more, or disloyalty to the government.
  • Deactivation Warning: Failure to vote in two (2) successive regular elections will deactivate your registration.

Voter Registration Process

Registration is managed by the COMELEC's Election Registration Board (ERB). The process includes:

  • Process: Visit the Local COMELEC Office (or satellite site). Submit the CEF-1 form and a Valid ID.
  • Key Step: Complete the Biometrics capture (photo, prints, signature).
  • Crucial: Keep the Acknowledgement Receipt.
‚úã

Mandatory Biometrics & AES

Biometrics are critical for the Automated Election System (AES) integrity (RA 10367). Without them, your unique identity is not verified.

  • Principle: 'No Bio, No Vote!' If your record lacks biometrics, your status is deactivated, and you cannot cast a ballot.
  • COMELEC Role: The COMELEC is the independent constitutional body administering all election laws.

SMART Candidate Review Guide

Adopt an issue-based approach. Research their background against the following criteria:

  • S: Service-oriented: Commitment to public interest.
  • M: Moral Integrity: Track record (e.g., SALN compliance) and ethical standing.
  • A: Achievements & Track Record: Concrete evidence of competence.
  • R: Realistic Platforms: Feasible solutions to current problems.
  • T: Transparency & Accountability: Willingness to be open about funds and decisions.

I. National Positions (Voted for Nationwide)

A. Executive Branch (6-Year Term)

President (1)

Elected for a single six (6) year term. Leads the entire Executive Branch.

One Vote Only.

Vice President (1)

Elected for a six (6) year term. Voted for separately from the President.

One Vote Only.

B. Legislative Branch (National Congress)

Senator (12) - Upper House

Select Twelve (12) Senators. Six (6) year term, two (2) consecutive term limit.

Maximum 12 Votes.

District Representative (1)

Vote for One (1) District Rep. Three (3) year term, three (3) consecutive term limit.

One Vote Only.

Party-List System (1)

Vote for One (1) Organization only. Represents marginalized sectors.

One Vote Only.
II. Local Positions (Voted for by Locality)

C. Provincial Executive Branch (3-Year Term)

Governor (1)

Leads the Provincial Executive affairs. Three (3) year term, three (3) consecutive term limit.

One Vote Only.

Vice Governor (1)

Presides over the Sangguniang Panlalawigan (Provincial Board).

One Vote Only.

D. City/Municipal Executive Branch (3-Year Term)

Mayor (1)

Leads the City/Municipal executive affairs and local government unit management.

One Vote Only.

Vice Mayor (1)

Presides over the Sangguniang Panlungsod / Bayan (Council).

One Vote Only.

E. Local Legislative Branch (Council)

Provincial Board/Councilors (SB/SP/SB)

Local Legislative officials. They enact local ordinances, approve budgets, and oversee the local executive branch.

Check local guidelines for the exact number of votes allowed.

Locate your precinct number before election day. You must SHADE THE OVAL COMPLETELY so the VCM can read your vote.

üõë CRITICAL PILLARS OF VOTERS' EDUCATION

An informed electorate is the bedrock of a robust Philippine democracy. Citizen-voters must be equipped with knowledge of their rights, the electoral process, and the issues at stake.

The Right to Suffrage & Registration

Citizens must know the legal qualifications for voting (e.g., at least 18 years of age, 6 months residency) and the proper process for **voter registration**, transfer, and reactivation, primarily administered by the Commission on Elections (**COMELEC**).

This includes awareness of registration deadlines and the importance of biometric validation to prevent disenfranchisement.

Source: 1987 Philippine Constitution, Article V & COMELEC Guidelines
Electoral System & Procedures (The How-To)

Understanding the difference between the **plurality system** (first-past-the-post) used for major positions and the **party-list system** is crucial. Voters need hands-on knowledge of the automated election system, including how to properly fill out the ballot and the role of the Vote Counting Machines (**VCMs**).

Knowing the number of votes allowed per position (e.g., 1 President, 1 Vice President, 12 Senators, etc.) is essential to avoid **"stray votes"** or overvoting.

Source: Omnibus Election Code, RA 9369 (Automated Election Law)
Informed Choice & Critical Thinking

The goal is to move voters beyond "popularity politics" and instead focus on a candidate's **platform, track record, and qualifications**. This section addresses perennial issues like **disinformation/fake news** and the pervasive challenge of **vote-buying** (money politics), encouraging a vote based on conscience and national interest.

Civic education includes understanding the roles and powers of the offices to be filled (Executive, Legislative, Local Government).

Source: Various Civil Society Organizations (e.g., NAMFREL, PPCRV) Reports
Election Watch & Accountability

Citizens have a role as poll watchers and are responsible for reporting instances of **electoral fraud, violence, or intimidation**. Awareness of the Fair Election Act, campaign finance rules, and the ability to file official complaints helps safeguard the sanctity of the ballot.

This also covers the importance of **voter turnout** and ensuring that marginalized groups (like PWDs, IPs, and Senior Citizens) have **accessible polling places** (RA 10366).

Source: COMELEC Resolutions, RA 10366 on Accessible Polling Places
